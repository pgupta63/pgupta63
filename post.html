Applying GIS

Map 1: Mapping Spatial Distribution

The purpose of this map was to apply several data manipulation skills to present the spatial distribution of density of homicides in Chicago by police district. This data was presented as a choropleth map in the appropriate UTM coordinate system, and also included a legend, scale bar, and north arrow. In order to organize the data for this problem, it was important to apply a variety of skills such as map joining, quantifying, summarizing, and many more. The final result is a map that relays technical information about homicide densities in Chicago, as well as an informative map that can be used for better city safety planning, funding, etc.

This map applies the skills of quantifying and summarizing data sets, as well as joining data for better organization and to relate the different data sets. Another skill acquired was the use of joined data and a the field calculator to apply and calculate the data appropriately to find various densities. The shapefiles were added onto ArcGIS Pro, where the Selected_Crimes and Police_Districts were summarized and joined to give the districts with homicide cases. A field for area was added to the Police_Districts file, and then the calculate tool was used to find the density of the homicide cases by district.
